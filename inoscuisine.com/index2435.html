History of Ile Flottante Recipe

Through the history of Ile flottante is not known, because of its rustic flavor it is assumed to have originated in the French countryside. It is also very popular in the cities and towns of France and can be found being served in most French bistros. Floating Island has been a popular dessert in France for a long time. The floating island tastes best when freshly made and served cold but also tastes good at room temperature.

Health and Nutrition Facts of the Flottante Dessert

Ile Flottante is a very light and low fat dessert. The custard does contain some calorie but it is very less compared to other desserts. Ile Flottante can be consumed by people with diabetes and heart disease.

]]> <![CDATA[Beef Paillard with Café De Paris Butter]]> VIANDE BOVINE PAILLARD AVEC BEURRE CAFÉ DE PARIS
Beef Paillard with Café De Paris Butter

Palliard is an old term used to describe cuts of meat that are thinly sliced or lightly pounded into flattened pieces that are then grilled or sautéed very quickly. Today the term more commonly used to describe this thin cut is cutlet. Paillards are made from boneless slices of chicken, turkey, veal, beef, and pork. The thin slices are generally cut from larger pieces of meat but supermarkets now have a variety of thinly sliced meats that can be used for paillards, eliminating the need to cut the thin slice before pounding.

Each type of meat has different cuts that are used for making cutlets / paillards. Chicken and turkey cutlets are made from the breast meat, veal cutlets are generally slices of meat from the leg or shoulder, beef cutlets can be slices from the tenderloin, round or sirloin, and pork cutlets are generally slices of meat from the loin or the leg.
